  13. Which of the following is not logical implication?( Marks: -0.33 )
    1.   P⋀Q⟹P↔Q
    2.   P↔Q⟹Q→P
    3.   P↔Q⟹P→~Q
      Explanation:
      If P⟹Q means P→Q is a tautology
      If P→Q is not a tautology that means it is not a logical implication
      Option (a):
      ■(P⋀Q@LHS)⟹■(P↔Q@RHS)
      Whenever P and Q is True, the LHS is true and RHS is also true.
      (If LHS is True for some P and Q then RHS must be True for the same P and Q)
      So, P⋀Q⟹P↔Q is a logical implication.
      Option (b):
      Whenever bi-conditional is True, conditional is also True.
      P↔Q⟹Q→P
      Option (c):
      If P and Q is True then P↔Q is true. But P→~Q is False. So P↔Q⟹P→~Q is not a Tautology.
      ∴P↔Q⟹P→~Q is not a logical implication.
      Option (d):
      If P is True and Q is False. Then ~(P→Q) is true. If Q is false then ~Q true.
      So, ~(P→Q)⟹~Q is a logical implication.
    4.   ~(P→Q)⟹~Q

  28. Let P(x) be the statement “x can speak Hindi” and let Q(x) be the statement “x knows the computer language Java”. The domain for quantifiers consists of all students of your school. What is the correct translation of the following statement into mathematical logic?
    “No student at your school can speak Hindi or knows Java”.( Marks: 2.00 )
    1.   ∃x (P(x)⋀Q(x) )
    2.   ∀x (P(x)⋁Q(x) )
    3.   ∃x ((~P(x)⋀(~Q(x) )
    4.   ∀x ((~P(x) )⋀(~Q(x) )
      Explanation:
      Let us consider the following statement
      “There is a student at your school can speak Hindi or knows Java”. This statement is represented as
      ∃x (P(x)⋁Q(x) )
      (we introduce a variable x whose domain consists of all students at school)
      ∃_x (P(x)⋁Q(x) )epresents atleast one student who can speak Hindi (or) who knows Java (or) who have both talents.
      The negation of the expression ∃x (P(x)⋁Q(x) )
      “It is not the case that there is a student at your school can speak Hindi or Java.”
      The above sentence can be modified as
      “No student at your school can speak Hindi or knows Java.”
      The mathematical logic for the statement is
      “No student at your school can speak Hindi or knows Java” is
      ~∃_x (P(x)⋁Q(x) )
      ⟺∀_x~(P(x)⋁Q(x) )
      ⟺∀x (~P(x)⋀(~Q(x) ) ) (∵ By De Morgan’s Law)
      Meaning of option A:
      There is a student at your school who can speak Hindi and who knows Java.
      Meaning of option B:
      Every student at your school either can speak Hindi or knows Java.
      Meaning of option C:
      There is a student at your school who cannot speak Hindi and who doesn’t know Java.
