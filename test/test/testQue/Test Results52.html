  11. Harry and Ron are playing a game with two six-sided dice, each number 1 to 6, they each toss a dice at the same time. If the product of two numbers on the two dice is bigger than or equal to 25 Ron wins the game.
    What is the probability that Ron wins the game?( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   1⁄9
      Explanation:
      Let X1 be the number that shows on Harry’s die and X2 the number on Ron’s. The possible values of X1 and X2 are
      X1∈{1,2,3,4,5,6}
      And
      X2∈{1,2,3,4,5,6}
      Then the possible values X=X1×X2
      X∈{1,2,3,4,5,6,8,9,10,12,15,16,18,20,24,25,30,36}
      Ron wins the game X≥25 or equivalently
      X=25 or 30 or 36
      P(X=25)=P(X1=5)×P(X2=5)=1/6×1/6=1/36
      P(X=30)=P(X1=5)×P(X2=6)+P(X1=6)×P(X2=5)=1/6×1/6+1/6×1/6=1/18
      P(X=36)=P(X1=6)×P(X2=6)=1/6×1/6=1/36
      P(X≥25)=P(X=25)+P(X=30)+P(X=36)=1/36+1/18+1/36=4/36=1/9
