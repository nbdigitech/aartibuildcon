  1. Choose the most appropriate word from the options given below to complete the following sentence.
    Vijay ________ really fast. Last year he was only half this height.( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   Will grow
    2.   Grew
    3.   Grows
    4.   Has grown
      Explanation:
      For the given sentence, we need a tense that refers to the action beginning at sometime in the past. Let’s try to rule out the options and find out the best fits in.
      (a) Will grow → future tense does not fit the sentence because in this context we need a tense that connects the past events to the present.
      (b) Grew → past tense would only be possible here if Vijay cannot grow any more. So, this is wrong.
      (c) Grows → present simple doesn’t fit the given sentence. But, present continuous tense would be possible here, i.e., Vijay is growing very fast.
      (d) Has grown → Vijay is still growing. So, there is a clear connection to the present. That’s why the present perfect tense is needed, i.e., an action beginning at sometime in the past and continuing up to the present moment. So, this is the correct choice.

    1.   6 days
    2.   6(2/3) days
    3.   6(4/5)days
      Explanation:
      A can complete a piece of work in 10 days
      B can complete the same work in 15 days
      C can complete the same work in 20 days
      Let he total work = LCM (A, B, C) units
      = 60 units

      At the most two people can work on each day and nobody works more than two consecutive days. That means A and B on first day, B and C on second day, C and A on third day, A and B on fourth day and so on.

      In the three days, the total work done = (A+B) + (B+C) + (C+A)
      = 2(A+B+C)
      = 26 units
      In 6 days 52 units of work be completed on 7th day A and B can finish 10 units but the remaining work is 60 – 52 = 8 units

      10 units can complete in 1 day
      8 units can complete in -----?
      =(8/10) days = (4/5)days
      So, minimum number of days that will take to finish the work =6(4/5)days
    4.   7 days
