  4. 1 Kg of wheat and 1 Kg of rice together cost Rs. 95. If the price of wheat falls by 20% and that of rice rises by 30% the price of 1 kg each, combined comes to Rs. 90. The original price of wheat in Rs. per Kg is:( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   66
    2.   68
    3.   67
      Explanation:
      Let 1 kg of wheat cost Rs. W
      Let I kg of rice cost Rs. R.
      Cost of 1 kg wheat and 1 kg rice is Rs. 95
      W + R = 95 -----------------(1)
      If the price of wheat falls by 20% and that of rice rises by 30%. Then the combined cost is Rs. 90.
      80% of W + 130% of R = 90
      (80/100)W + (130/100)R = 90
      (8/10)W + (13/10)R = 90
      8W + 13R = 900 -------------(2)
      Multiply the eqn (1) by 13. Then we get 13 W + 13 R = 1235 ----------(3)
      Subtracting eqn (2) from eqn (3)
      5W = 335
      W = 67
      So, 1 kg of wheat cost is Rs. 67
    4.   71

  7. Select the pair that best expresses a relationship similar to that expressed in the pair.
    EXHAUSTING : REJUVENATING( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   Vexing : Irritating
    2.   Contaminate : Pollute
    3.   Cruel : Ruthless
    4.   Disturbing : Soothing
      Explanation:
      Relationship between the given pair: ANTONYM VARIANT
      Rejuvenating is to make (someone or something) look or feel better, younger, or more vital, i.e., to kick-start or revive. Thus, exhausting is the opposite of rejuvenating.
      Option (a):
      Vexing is to irritate, bother or frustrate. So, vexing and irritating are synonyms to each other.
      Option (b):
      Contaminate is to make (something) impure by exposure to or addition of poisonous or polluting substance, i.e., to pollute.
      Option (c):
      Cruel is to willfully causing pain or suffering to others, or feeling no concern about it, i.e., being brutal or ruthless.
      Option (d):
      Disturbing refers to trouble emotionally (or) mentally; upset or worrying and soothing refers to reducing pain or discomfort, having a gently calming effect. Thus, soothing is an antonym for disturbing.
      Therefore, from the given explanation, the relationship that best expresses a relationship similar to Exhausting: Rejuvenating is Disturbing: Soothing.


  8. Which part (underlined) of the following passage contains a mistake?
    I saw a very nice dress in the exhibition at the weekend. But I didn’t have enough money with me, so I didn’t bought it.( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   didn’t have
    2.   saw
    3.   didn’t bought
      Explanation:
      Explanation:
      Option (a): (Didn’t have)
      This isn't a mistake because we are talking about the state that existed (or) the things that happened before now, i.e., last weekend and the past simple is the correct tense for representing such actions.
      Option (b): (Saw)
      In this context, we are referring to an action that has happened or have finished before now, i.e., last weekend and the past simple is the correct tense for representing such actions. So, this isn’t a mistake.
      Option (c): (didn’t bought):
      Since all the actions took place in finished time, i.e., last weekend. So all the verbs should be in the past simple tense, i.e.,didn’t buy(verb + v1). So, it should be “I didn’t buy it”.
    4.   no mistake
