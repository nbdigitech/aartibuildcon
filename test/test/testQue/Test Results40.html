  7. The Yoga system is divided into two principal parts, Hatha and Raja Yoga. Hatha Yoga deals principally with the physiological part of man with a view to establish his health and train his will. The processes prescribed to arrive at this end are so difficult that only a few resolute souls go through all the stages of its practice. Many have failed and some have died in the attempt. It is therefore strongly denounced by all the philosophers. The most illustrious Shankaracharya has remarked in his treatise called Aparokshanubhuti that “the system of Hatha Yoga was intended for those whose worldly desires are not pacified or uprooted.”

    Which of the statements can be inferred from the above passage?( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   Anyone who attempts Hatha Yoga dies
    2.   Philosophers strongly recommend Hatha Yoga.
    3.   The processes included in hatha yoga are very difficult.
      Explanation:
      Option (A) :
      This statement cannot be inferred from the passage because not all who attempts hatha yoga dies (“Many have failed and some have died in the attempt”).
      Option (B):
      This statement cannot be inferred as it is given in the passage that “It is therefore strongly denounced by all the philosophers”. Denounce means to “publicly declare to be wrong, evil or criticize” which contradicts the given statement.
      Option (D):
      This statement cannot be inferred from the passage as it is given that, “the system of Hatha Yoga was intended for those, whose worldly desires are not pacified or uprooted” but not for everyone.
      Option (C):
      In the passage, it is given that “The processes (hatha yoga) prescribed to arrive at this end are so difficult”. Therefore, the given statement can be inferred directly from the passage.
    4.   Shankaracharya has remarked that the system of Hatha Yoga is for everyone.

  11. A mixture contains 80% acid and the rest water. Part of the mixture that should be removed and replaced by the same amount of water to make the ratio of acid and water 4:3 is( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   1/3rd
    2.   3/7th
    3.   2/3rd
    4.   2/7th
      Explanation:
      cid in mixture=80%= 4/5
      Water in mixture=20%=1/5
      Let xth part of the mixture remove.
      Part of Acid in mixture left=4/5-4/5 x
      Part of water in mixture left=1/5-1/5 x
      xth Part of the water replaced, then
      (4/5-4/5 x)/(1/5-1/5 x+x)=4/3
      ⇒(4-4x)/(1-x+5x)=4/3
      ⇒12-12x=4+16x
      ⇒x=8/28=2/7
      ⇒x=2/7th


  12. In a certain code, ‘he is waiting there’ is written as ‘la pa ro ta’, ‘there is the train’ is written as 'zo ro ji la','waiting in the station' is written as 'ma ta fu ji'and
    'is this a station' is written as 'fu bi ro vi'. Which of the following represents 'the train station'?( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   zo la ma
    2.   fu ji ta
    3.   fu ji zo
      Explanation:
      ‘There is the train’is written as 'zo ro ji la', 'waiting in the station' is written as 'ma ta fu ji'.
      The common word from the above sentences is 'the' and the common code is 'ji'.
      So, 'the' is coded as 'ji'.
      'Waiting in the station'is coded as 'ma ta fu ji' and 'is this a station' is coded as 'fu bi ro vi'. The common word is 'station' and the code for station is 'fu'.
      From the sentences, ‘he is waiting there’and ‘there is the train’, the sentence 'there is' coded as 'la ro'.
      ∴Train is coded as 'zo'.
      Hence, 'the train station'⟶fu ji zo.
    4.   ro zo fu

  14. Smallest angle of a triangle is equal to two-third the smallest angle of a quadrilateral. The ratio between the angles of the quadrilateral is 3:4:5:6. Largest angle of the triangle is twice its smallest angle. What is the sum of second largest angle of the triangle and second largest angle of the quadrilateral?( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   140°
    2.   160°
      Explanation:
      Let the angles of the quadrilateral be 3x,4x,5x and 6x respectively.
      ⇒3x+4x+5x+6x=360°
      18x=360°
      x=20°
      ∴Smallest angle of the triangle=3×2/3×20°
      =40°
      ∴Largest angle of the triangle=40°×2
      =80°
      ∴Second largest angle of the triangle=180°-(40°+80°)
      =60°
      The second largest angle of the quadrilateral=5x=100°
      Hence,required sum=60°+100°=160°
    3.   180°
    4.   200°
