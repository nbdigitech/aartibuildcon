  9. Sachin, Dravid and Ganguly are visiting the Dhoni’s family who are staying 400 km away. Each of their walking speeds is 20 kmph. Initially, Dravid and Ganguly travel in a car at the rate of 100 kmph and Sachin walks the distance. After a while, Ganguly gets off the car as he feels nauseated and walks the rest of the distance to the house. Dravid goes back in the car to fetch Sachin and they all reach the house at the same time. What was the entire time involved in travelling?( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   6 hours
    2.   8 hours
      Explanation:
      Let ‘x’ be the number of hours Sachin walks at 20 kmph, x will be the number of hours Dravid and Ganguly travelled by car at 100 kmph.
      Let ‘y’ be the number of hours that Ganguly walks till Dravid reaches Sachin.
      Let ‘z’ be the number of hours when Dravid and Sachin travel in the car till the destination.
      The total distance traversed by each of them can be evaluated as follows:
      Dravid=100x-100y+100z=400 ⟶①
      Sachin=20x+20y+100z=400 ⟶②
      Ganguly=100x+20y+20z=400 ⟶③
      Dividing equation ① by 100, equation ② by 20 and equation ③ by 20, we get
      x-y+z=4⟶④
      x+y+5z=20⟶⑤
      5x+y+z=20⟶⑥
      By adding ④&⑤, we get
      2x+6z=24
      x+3z=12
      3x+9z=36⟶⑦
      By adding ④&⑥, we get
      3x+z=12⟶⑧
      Subtracting ⑦ ─ ⑧, we get
      8z=24 ⟹z=3
      Then x=3
      Substitute the value of x and z in eqn④
      y=2
      Entire time involved in travelling =x+y+z
      =3+2+3
      =8 hours
    3.   9 hours
    4.   10 hours

  12. Early data on seat-belt use showed that seat-belt wearers were less likely to be killed in road-accidents. Hence, it was initially believed that wearing a seat-belt increased survival chances in an accident. But what the analysts had failed to see was, that cautious drivers were more likely to wear the belts and were also less likely to cause ‘big accidents’, while reckless drivers were more likely to cause ‘big’ accidents and were less likely to wear belts.

    Which of the statements below cannot be inferred from this passage?( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   Seat-belt wearers are less likely to be killed in the road accidents.
    2.   Analysts have failed to take the cautious & reckless drivers into consideration in the early data on seat-belt use.
    3.   Cautious drivers cause less accidents compared to reckless drivers
    4.   Both cautious and reckless drivers are equally likely to wear seat belts.
      Explanation:
      Option (a):
      This statement can be directly inferred from the first sentence of the given passage, “Seat-belt wearers are less likely to be killed in road accidents”.
      Option (b)& (c):
      In the given passage, it is mentioned that “analysts had failed to see was, that cautious drivers were more likely to wear the belts and were also less likely to cause ‘big accidents’, while reckless drivers were more likely to cause ‘big’ accidents and were less likely to wear belts” which can be interpreted as “In the early data on seatbelt use, analysts had failed to take the cautious & reckless drivers into consideration and cautious drivers cause less accidents compared to reckless drivers”. So, option (b) & option(c) can be inferred from the passage.
      Option (d):
      This statement is not true as it is given in the passage that “cautious drivers were more likely to wear seat belts and reckless drivers were less likely to wear seat belts”. So, this option cannot be inferred from the given passage.

  14. Common data question:
    Study the table carefully to answer the questions given below.
    Percentage of men employed in five departments of five organizations.

    Find the total number of women working in the HR and the IT departments in company C.( Marks: 0.00 )
    1.   2614
    2.   2704
    3.   5796
    4.   2604
      Explanation:
      Total number of employees in each department of organization C is 4200.
      Percentage of men employed in IT Department of company C is 76%. So, women employed in IT Department of company C are 24%.
      Percentage of men employed in HR Department of company C is 62%. So, women employed in HR Department of company C are 38%.
      Therefore, total number of women working in the IT and HR Department
      = (24 + 38) % of 4200
      = 62% of 4200
      = (62/100) X 4200
      = 2604
